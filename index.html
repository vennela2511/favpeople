My Favourite People

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, whose full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, stands as one of India’s most revered scientists and leaders. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family, he overcame early financial hardships through determination and hard work. Kalam’s academic journey began at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, where he studied Physics, followed by Aeronautical Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. Kalam’s career began at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958, and he later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he played a pivotal role in developing India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-3), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980, marking a major milestone in India’s space program. His leadership in missile technology, particularly with the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earned him the title “Missile Man of India”. He was instrumental in advancing India’s nuclear capabilities, notably during the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998

Rohit Gurunath Sharma

Rohit GurunathSharma, Rohit Gurunath Sharma, born on April 30, 1987, in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, is a prominent Indian international cricketer and one of the most successful batsmen in the history of the game. Known by his nickname "Hitman," Rohit Sharma has earned a reputation for his elegant and powerful batting style, particularly in limited-overs cricket. Sharma began his cricket journey in 1999 when he joined a cricket camp, overcoming modest family circumstances as he was raised by his grandparents and uncle due to his father's low income. His early promise was evident when he scored 142 runs in the 2005 Deodhar Trophy, playing for West Zone against North Zone.He made his international debut for India in 2007 and gradually established himself as a key player. Rohit Sharma is the only batsman in the world to have scored three double centuries in One Day Internationals (ODIs), a record unmatched by any other player. His highest individual ODI score is 264 runs against Sri Lanka, the highest ever in ODI cricket. He is also the fastest Indian opener to reach 4,000 ODI runs.

Mother Teresa

Mother Teresa, born Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu on August 26, 1910, in Skopje (now North Macedonia), was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary renowned for her lifelong dedication to the poor and sick. Coming from an Albanian Catholic family, she was deeply influenced by her mother's compassion and commitment to charity. At 18, she left home to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland and soon moved to India, where she began teaching in Kolkata.In 1948, moved by the extreme poverty and suffering she witnessed, Mother Teresa started working directly with the destitute in Kolkata's slums. In 1950, she founded the Missionaries of Charity, a religious congregation focused on caring for "the poorest of the poor," including the sick, orphaned, and dying. Her work earned her international recognition, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979 and India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, in 1980. Mother Teresa's life exemplified selfless service, humility, and compassion. She walked barefoot to reach those in need and spent hours in prayer, drawing strength from her faith. She passed away in 1997 in Kolkata and was canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta in 2016. Her legacy continues to inspire millions worldwide to serve humanity with love and kindness.

Elon Musk/h1>

Elon Musk, born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa, is a visionary entrepreneur and the world's richest person as of 2025, with a net worth estimated at over $420 billion. He holds degrees in physics and economics from the University of Pennsylvania and briefly attended Stanford University before pursuing business ventures. Musk co-founded Zip2, an online city guide software company, which he sold for millions, and later founded X.com, an online payment company that evolved into PayPal and was acquired by eBay in 2002. In 2002, he founded SpaceX with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling Mars colonization; SpaceX has since revolutionized spaceflight with reusable rockets and numerous successful launches, dominating the global launch market by 2024. He joined Tesla Motors in 2004, becoming CEO and product architect in 2008, transforming it into a leader in electric vehicles and sustainable energy solutions, including solar power and battery storage.
